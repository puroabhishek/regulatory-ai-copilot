1. Purpose and Scope of Application
1.1 Purpose
The purpose of this Customer Onboarding Policy ("Policy") is to establish a clear and comprehensive
framework for the onboarding of micro, small, and medium enterprises (“MSMEs” or “Customers”) by
Madad Financial Technologies, a company incorporated under the laws of the Qatar Financial Centre
(“QFC”) bearing QFC license number 03602 (the “Company”)
The Company is inter alia engaged in operating a digital platform offering invoice discounting
solutions tailored to the needs of MSMEs (“Services”). The Services are provided to Customers
involved in industries, including but not limited to, trading, manufacturing and services (“Business
Industries”). This Policy aims to ensure that all Customer onboarding activities are conducted in strict
compliance with the applicable laws, regulations, and guidelines issued by the Qatar Central Bank
(“QCB”) and the Qatar Financial Centre Authority (“QFCRA”), as maybe amended from time to time. It
is intended to protect the integrity of the Company’s operations by mitigating risks related to money
laundering, terrorist financing, fraud, and other illegal activities. This Policy must be considered
alongside the Anti-Money Laundering and Counter-Terrorist Financing (“AML/CTF”) Policy,
(“AML/CTF Policy”)
This Policy shall enter into force only upon formal approval by the Company’s Board of Directors.
The Board is responsible for reviewing, endorsing, and approving this Policy in accordance with the
governance requirements set by the Qatar Central Bank (QCB).
The Policy has been endorsed by Senior Management and will be submitted to the Board of Directors
for final approval immediately upon completion of all QCB-mandated updates.
1.2 Scope
This Policy further aims to promote transparency, accountability, and due diligence in the Customer
onboarding process by clearly defining the procedures for Customer identification, verification, risk
assessment, and acceptance. By adhering to this Policy, the Company seeks to uphold its commitment
to regulatory compliance, Customer protection, and the highest standards of ethical business conduct.
This Policy shall be effective from the date of its approval by the Company’s Board of Directors and
shall apply universally to all business units, departments, employees, agents, consultants, and any
other representatives of the Company (collectively referred to as "Personnel") who are involved,
directly or indirectly, in the process of onboarding Customers. This includes, but is not limited to:

2. Customer Due Diligence and Know Your Customers (KYC) Requirements
The Company is committed to conducting Customer Due Diligence (“CDD”) and Know Your Customer
(“KYC”) procedures in accordance with the requirements set forth by QCB and other applicable
regulations. Company’s senior management to check the decision on eKYC process through proper
assessment and check the process to monitor the services.
Company’s senior management are responsible for the assessment, understanding and monitoring of
the Entity’s reliance on e-KYC for material services.
In case eKYC services are outsourced, Company to obtain QCB approval for e-KYC third party.
Company recognize that different types of legal entities, arrangements, and facilities—such as
companies, groups, partnerships, trusts, nominee shareholdings, and powers of attorney—pose
distinct risks of financial crime, including money laundering and terrorist financing. These structures
can obscure the identity of beneficial owners, facilitate complex ownership arrangements, or allow
for misuse of funds. Therefore, Company applies enhanced scrutiny to these entities by conducting
thorough beneficial ownership verification, understanding the ownership and control structure, and
identifying any nominee relationships that may present heightened risk.

2.1 Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Process
CDD is the process used by the Company to identify and verify the identity of its customers. This
process includes:
• The CDD starts at the signup stage itself where customer to go through milti-factor
authentication measures like OTP verification of the phone number
• Collecting customers information via digital onboarding form. This form required upload of all
company related documents, for verifying their identity, creditworthiness, financial health, and
good standing using reliable and independent documents, any other data or information.
• Identifying and verifying the identity of ultimate beneficial owners (UBO) of Customers to
ensure that the Company knows who they are. Identifying the UBO identity through
performing a live scan of the documents
• The UBOs verification to be conducted with liveness check like checking the 3D depth, facial
expression, movements and face match (to the ID) through the camera and ensuring that KYC
is not done on still images
• Identifying persons acting on behalf of a Customer, verifying their identity, and confirming their
authority to act on behalf of the Customer; obtaining an acknowledgement that the person is
the ultimate beneficial owner; screening Customers to ensure compliance with National
Counter Terrorism Committee (NCTC) sanctions, United Nations sanctions, and other
applicable sanctions; obtaining sufficient information to understand the purpose and nature of
the business relationship; understanding the flow of funds, including their source and ultimate
destination
• Conducting ongoing monitoring of the business relationship and transaction activities;
ensuring that business activities align with the Company’s knowledge of the Customer; and
maintaining up-to-date information on the Customer
• The Company shall conduct ongoing monitoring of the business relationship, including:
o Scrutiny of transactions undertaken throughout the course of that relationship to
ensure they are consistent with the knowledge of the Customer, their business, and
risk profile.
o Ensuring that documents, data, or information held are kept up to date by undertaking
reviews of existing records.
o Monitoring for unusual or suspicious transactions and escalating such transactions to
the Compliance Officer

Customer that he/she will update the information (any information related to the KYC process)
as soon as it happens or upon request from the company
• The Company shall review and update Customer Data at regular intervals determined by the
Customer's risk rating, with high-risk Customers reviewed more frequently. Additionally, a

2.2 Triggers to conduct Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Process
CDD must be conducted whenever required by the circumstances of the Company’s relationship with
the Customer. This includes:
• CDD should be finalized before initiating any Service or transactions with a Customer.
• Establishing a Business Relationship: CDD must be performed when the Company first
establishes a business relationship with a customer.

• High-Value Transactions: CDD is required when conducting a transaction for an occasional
Customer where the transaction value exceeds QAR 55,000 or its equivalent in foreign
currency.
• Linked Transactions: CDD must be conducted when a series of transactions for an occasional
Customer occur closely in time or appear to be linked, with the aggregate value exceeding
QAR 55,000, or its equivalent in foreign currency.
• Change in Customer Circumstances: CDD is required when there is a significant change in the
Customer’s circumstances or business operations.
• Significant Changes in Business: CDD must be undertaken when there is a significant change
in the Customer’s Business Industry/activities or operational structure.
• Doubt About Information: CDD is required when the Company has doubts about the accuracy
or completeness of existing information held on a Customer.
• Suspicion of Money Laundering (“ML”) or Terrorism Financing (“TF”): CDD must be conducted
when there are suspicions of ML or TF related to the Customer or their transactions.
• Physical presence of the customer is needed at the time of commencing the business
relationship, which will be at the time of contract signatures. This will be driven and in line
with the Financial Institution’s policy who are ultimate provider of the credit line / funds.
2.3 Customers who failed onboarding
Company to follow following process for the Customer who have failed onboarding:
• Company shall provide the Customer clear guidance on the specific reason for failure and the
corrective actions required
• Customers who have failed onboarding are permitted to re-apply after 3 months from the
date of their onboarding rejection
• The customer must rectify the reasons of onboarding failures
• Each re-registration attempt to be treated as a new onboarding process, and all onboarding
checks (KYC/KYB/AML etc.) to be completed afresh
• The Compliance Officer (or designated official) will oversee re-registration cases and ensure
adherence to the policy

independent sources. The Customer Data must be current as of the time it is obtained. All documents

obtain illicitly. These include, but are not limited to, government-issued identity cards, passports,
reports from independent business and company registries, audited annual reports, and other reliable

4. Record Keeping
All records relating to Customer identification, verification, CDD, and EDD (as defined below) ,
transaction history, correspondences with Customers and reports of unusual transactions shall be
stored in encrypted format and secured using multi-factor authentication (MFA). Daily backups shall
be conducted to prevent data loss. Access to such records shall be restricted to authorized Personnel

• Copies or records of official identification documents, including but not limited to passports
and identity cards of authorized signatories, beneficial owners, and agents; commercial
registration extracts; information regarding the Customer’s business activities; and
documentation evidencing the Customer’s financial and legal good standing. The Company
shall also obtain any additional documents as may be required under the applicable laws and
regulations of QFC and QCB.

basis.
• Account files and business correspondence.
• Analysis performed by the Company, including inquiries to understand the background and
purpose of complex, unusual, or large transactions.
CDD records must encompass all relevant transaction records, both domestic and international, to
enable the Company to promptly respond to information requests from competent authorities. These
records must be detailed enough to reconstruct individual transactions, including the amounts and
types of currency involved if applicable, to provide evidence for potential criminal investigations if
necessary.

6 Politically Exposed Person (PEP)
A PEP is someone who holds or has held a significant public role, either within the State of Qatar, a
foreign country, or an international organization. The Company shall implement effective risk
management systems to identify whether the person controlling the Customer, or the beneficial
owner/authorized signatory of the Customer is a PEP.
The Company should use sound judgment in identifying PEPs and consider various sources of
information, including:
• Internal assessments;
• Information provided by the Customer;
• Knowledge from local staff;
• Internet searches;
• Commercial databases;
• Obtaining certified copies of asset declarations from the PEP;
• Notarized confirmation of political office dates (if applicable); and
• Other publicly available information.
The Company should be cautious of the limitations inherent in commercial databases. The mere
presence or absence of a name in a database should not be the sole factor in determining PEP status,
as definitions used by database providers may differ from those used by the Company.
Company shall consider the reputational risks associated with establishing a relationship with a
Politically Exposed Person (PEP). This evaluation will include assessing the nature of the PEP’s
current or former public position, any known adverse media or involvement in controversies, and the
potential impact on Company’s reputation and standing with regulatory authorities.
The reasons for classifying someone as a PEP should be clearly documented.
When a PEP is identified, EDD (as defined below) must be conducted, which includes:
• Securing senior management approval to initiate or continue the relationship (or for existing
customers, to review and approve continuation or termination of the relationship annually).
• Verifying the source of wealth and funds.
• Implementing enhanced ongoing monitoring of the business relationship.
These additional measures should also apply to the family members or close associates of PEPs.
When assessing whether an individual is a close associate of a PEP, the Company may evaluate

watermarking to detect document tampering or duplication.

associated with a high-risk jurisdiction, as defined under FATF guidance and the Company’s

The Company should take reasonable steps, aligned with the Customer's risk profile and the nature
of the business relationship, to identify the source of the Customer’s wealth and funds. Company must
verify the source of wealth and funds using reliable, independent documents, data, or information,

The source of wealth refers to the origin of the Customer’s and beneficial owner’s total wealth (i.e.,
their overall assets). It addresses how the Customer and beneficial owner acquired their wealth, rather
than just identifying their assets. Source of wealth information should provide an indication of the
expected size of the Customer’s wealth and the manner in which it was acquired. Even if the Company

for establishing the source of wealth include evidence of title, copies of trust deeds, audited financial

records, tax returns, and bank statements.
The source of funds pertains to the origin of the specific funds or assets used by the Customer in their
business relationship with the Company (e.g., amounts being invested, deposited, or transferred). To
mitigate the risk of funds being from criminal activities, the Company should examine the activity that
generated the funds rather than solely identifying the financial institution from which the funds were
transferred. The type and depth of inquiries required to establish the source of funds will vary based
on the customer’s risk profile and the nature of the transaction.
The Company also incorporates verification of the source of funds as part of its Customer Due
Diligence process, in accordance with the customer's risk level. This includes reviewing supporting
documentation, where necessary, to confirm that the funds being used for a transaction or business
relationship are consistent with the Customer’s declared profile and financial activity.
Additionally, the Company defines "occasional customers" as those who perform a one-off transaction

primarily designed for repeated usage and active business engagement, any customer who performs
a single invoice discounting transaction and does not return for further activity within 180 days is

The findings of the annual AML/CTF audit shall be formally documented in an audit report. This report
must detail any identified compliance gaps, deficiencies, or areas requiring improvement, together
with recommended corrective actions. The audit report shall be presented to senior management and
the Board of Directors within thirty (30) days of the audit’s completion. Records of the audit report
and subsequent corrective actions shall be retained by the Compliance Department for at least ten
(10) years and made available to regulatory authorities upon request.
The Compliance Officer shall be responsible for ensuring that all recommended actions from the
annual audit are implemented within agreed timelines. Progress updates shall be periodically
provided to senior management and the Board of Directors until all issues are satisfactorily resolved.
18 Review and Amendments
This Policy shall be reviewed at least annually or more frequently as necessary to ensure its continued
effectiveness and compliance with QCB and QFCRA regulations, applicable laws, and industry best
practices. The review process will assess the Policy’s relevance, adequacy, and alignment with
regulatory requirements and operational needs.
Any amendments or updates to this Policy must receive prior approval from the Company’s senior
management. The approval process will ensure that all changes are thoroughly evaluated for legal,
regulatory, and operational implications before implementation.
Following approval, any amendments to the Policy will be communicated promptly to all relevant
stakeholders, including Personnel, customers, and regulatory bodies as applicable. The
communication will include details of the changes, the effective date, and any necessary actions
required to comply with the updated Policy.
Records of Policy reviews, amendments, and approvals will be maintained in accordance with legal
and regulatory requirements. These records will be available for inspection by regulatory authorities
and other relevant parties as required.
